The Best Dog-Friendly Travel Destinations and Tips for Traveling with Your Dog

Do you want to take a vacation but hate the idea of leaving your pup behind? Well, good news: there are plenty of travel destinations where dogs are not only welcome but celebrated! Traveling with a dog can be a blast, especially when you pick places that offer dog-friendly accommodations, activities, and dining options. And let’s face it, dogs make excellent travel companions. They never complain about your playlist, don’t hog the snacks (well, they try), and are always up for adventure.

In this post, we’ll cover the top dog-friendly travel spots, along with tips and tricks to make traveling with your furry friend smooth and stress-free. Get ready to load up the car, pack some treats, and hit the road!

Why Travel with Your Dog?

Let’s start with the basics: why take your dog along for the journey? There’s nothing like watching your dog’s face light up at new sights, smells, and sounds. Plus, having your dog with you can be a great conversation starter, helping you make new friends on the road (dogs have a way of bringing out the best in people). But the best part? No matter where you go, your pup will be there to share it with you.

Dog-Friendly Destinations You and Your Pup Will Love

1. Asheville, North Carolina: Mountain Adventures and Doggy Breweries

Asheville is a dream destination for outdoor enthusiasts and dog lovers alike. This charming mountain town is filled with hiking trails, rivers, and forests, all of which are dog-friendly. You can explore scenic spots like the Blue Ridge Parkway, which has several trails that are great for dogs, or take a stroll through the dog-friendly North Carolina Arboretum.

Doggie Fun Fact: Asheville has numerous dog-friendly breweries that let you bring your pup inside! If you’ve ever dreamed of sipping a craft beer while your dog lounges next to you, this is the place to be.

2. Portland, Oregon: The Dog Capital of the West

Portland might just be one of the most dog-friendly cities in the country. Known for its open-minded, nature-loving vibe, Portland has over 30 off-leash dog parks and countless dog-friendly restaurants, cafes, and shops. The city’s waterfront trails and nearby natural parks are ideal for scenic dog walks.

Pro tip: Don’t miss the Lucky Labrador Brewing Company, where you and your dog can both relax (though only you get to sample the local brews, sadly).

3. Austin, Texas: Where Everything’s Bigger (Including Dog Fun!)

Austin, the capital of Texas, has a vibrant dog-friendly scene. With its beautiful parks, lakes, and pet-friendly dining options, you and your pup will find endless activities. Zilker Park is a sprawling green space where dogs are free to roam off-leash. And if your dog likes water, you can even go paddleboarding together on Lady Bird Lake.

Local Highlight: Austin hosts the annual Dog Beard and Moustache Competition, where dogs and their owners dress up with matching facial hair. Who knew mustaches could look so good on a Schnauzer?

4. Carmel-by-the-Sea, California: A Coastal Haven for Canine Companions

This picturesque coastal town on California’s Monterey Peninsula is not only breathtakingly beautiful but also incredibly dog-friendly. Carmel Beach allows dogs off-leash, giving them free reign to dig in the sand, chase waves, or just nap in the sun. Many restaurants and shops in Carmel welcome dogs, and there’s even a special bakery just for pups.

Insider Tip: Some hotels in Carmel offer “yappy hour,” complete with treats and toys for your furry friend. Imagine you and your dog clinking glasses (or bowls) at sunset!

5. Bar Harbor, Maine: Nature Trails and Seaside Serenity

If you and your dog are craving a mix of mountains, forests, and seaside views, Bar Harbor is a fantastic choice. Nestled by Acadia National Park, Bar Harbor allows leashed dogs on most trails, including Cadillac Mountain, where you can catch a sunrise together. The town is dog-friendly, with several cafes and restaurants offering outdoor seating for you and your pup.

Adventure Alert: Acadia National Park is one of the few national parks in the U.S. that allows dogs on most trails, making it a must-visit for nature-loving dog owners.

Tips for Traveling with Your Dog

Now that you have some destinations in mind, here are some tips to make the journey enjoyable for both you and your pup. Because let’s be honest—traveling with a dog requires a little extra prep (unless you want to find yourself on a road trip with a dog who’s eaten all the snacks and left you with the crumbs).

1. Packing Essentials for Your Dog

Just like you have a travel checklist, your dog has one, too! Here are some must-haves for a smooth trip:

  • Water and food bowls: Collapsible bowls are great for saving space.
  • Leash and harness: Even if your destination has off-leash areas, bring a sturdy leash.
  • Poop bags: Lots of them. Nobody wants to be the person without a poop bag when nature calls.
  • Bed or blanket: Familiar bedding can make a new place feel more like home.
  • Toys and treats: For keeping them entertained and bribing them if needed.
  • Medical records and ID tags: Just in case. Many pet-friendly hotels and airlines require proof of vaccinations.

Bonus Tip: Bring a lint roller. Because, trust me, if you’re traveling with a dog, you’ll need it.

2. Crate Training for the Journey

Whether you’re flying or driving, a crate can help keep your dog safe and calm. Make sure it’s comfortable and has enough room for them to stand, turn around, and lie down. Plus, if they’re already crate-trained, the crate will feel like a familiar, secure space when they’re in an unfamiliar place.

Pro Tip: Crate training is also a great way to prevent them from “exploring” the car in ways you don’t want—like hopping into your lap while you’re trying to drive.

3. Keeping Your Dog Calm in the Car

Some dogs love car rides, while others… not so much. If your dog falls into the latter category, here are a few ideas:

  • Start small: Take short trips around the neighborhood before a long road trip to get them used to the motion.
  • Frequent breaks: Plan for breaks every 2-3 hours to let them stretch, sniff, and, well, take care of business.
  • Calming aids: A comfy bed, familiar toys, and even a canine calming spray can work wonders for anxious travelers.

Tips for Dog-Friendly Hotel Stays

Once you’ve reached your destination, you’ll need a place to stay. More hotels are becoming pet-friendly, but that doesn’t mean they all have the same policies. Here’s how to make sure your hotel experience is enjoyable for both you and your pup.

1. Check the Hotel’s Pet Policy

Some hotels allow dogs, but with restrictions, so read the fine print! Look for things like:

  • Weight limits: Some hotels restrict dogs over a certain size.
  • Additional fees: Many hotels charge a pet fee, which can range from a small nightly charge to a hefty deposit.
  • Room rules: Some hotels ask that dogs not be left alone in rooms, so you may need a plan if you want to go out for dinner.

Pro Tip: Always call ahead to confirm the details. Pet-friendly doesn’t always mean “completely open to all dogs.”

2. Doggie Room Service? Yes, Please!

Some hotels go above and beyond with pet amenities, offering everything from doggie room service to pet spa days. If you’re staying at a dog-friendly luxury hotel, check if they have any special services for four-legged guests. After all, why should you be the only one enjoying a treat?

Dog-Friendly Dining: Eating Out with Your Pup

Most dog-friendly cities have outdoor seating areas where you can enjoy a meal with your dog by your side. Here are some dining tips to ensure a smooth experience.

  1. Ask for a Table with Space: Try to get a spot with enough room for your dog to lie down comfortably. A little space also reduces the risk of “counter-surfing” from your pup (or at least makes it less tempting).
  2. Bring a Water Bowl: Some restaurants provide them, but it’s always good to be prepared.
  3. Practice Good Dog Etiquette: Not everyone loves dogs as much as you do (I know, shocking). Keeping your dog close and quiet will make sure everyone has a good time.

Insider Tip: Many breweries and cafes in dog-friendly areas offer special dog menus. Think biscuits, “doggie ice cream,” and more! Perfect for treating your pup without sacrificing your dessert.

Outdoor Adventures with Your Dog

If you’re traveling to a place with great outdoor spaces, there’s no better travel buddy than your dog. Hiking, beaches, parks, and even city tours can be fun with your pup.

  1. Check for Dog-Friendly Trails: Before heading out, double-check that the trails allow dogs. Some trails have restrictions, especially in national parks.
  2. Leash Rules: Even in dog-friendly areas, leashes are often required to protect both your dog and local wildlife.
  3. Watch for Paw Hazards: Hot sand, sharp rocks, and burrs can be tough on paws, so keep an eye out. If you’re heading into challenging terrain, you might want to invest in a set of doggie booties.

Final Words

Traveling with your dog can be one of the most fun and rewarding experiences—think of all the memories you’ll make together! While it might require a little extra planning, seeing your dog’s face light up in a new place is worth it. So grab the leash, pack some snacks, and get ready for an adventure you’ll both love.
